Camel

  • When a camel finally does find water, he can drink up to 40 gallons in one go.
  • Camels can completely shut their nostrils during sandstorms.
  • Mother camels carry their calves up to 14 months before giving birth.

Chameleons

  • Chameleons have eyes in the backs of their heads
  • Chameleons have good eyesight for a lizard
  • Unlike other lizards, chameleons do not regrow their tails.

Pigeons

  • Pigeons mate for life, and tend to raise two chicks at the same time.
  • Pigeons are highly sociable animals
  • They primarily feed on seeds, fruits, and plants.

teddy-bear

  • The term bear-hug was first recorded in 1846.
  • The Oxford English Dictionary dates the first use of the term teddy bear to 1906.
  • The official term for a teddy bear collector is an ‘arctophile’.

Pandas

  • Giant pandas are good at climbing trees and can also swim.
  • Pandas are "lazy" — eating and sleeping make their day.
  • An adult can eat 12–38 kilos of bamboo per day!

Cicada

  • Adult cicadas, sometimes called imagines, are usually between 2cm and 5cm long.
  • "Cicada" comes from the Latin, meaning "tree cricket."
  • Once they rise from the soil, these cicadas live for four to six weeks.
